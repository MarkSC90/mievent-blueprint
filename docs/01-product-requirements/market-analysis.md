# Purpose

This document analyses the current event management software market, identifies industry pain points and demonstrates the opportunity for MiEvent to become the operating system for professional event businesses.

This document exists to answer one question:

> **Why should MiEvent exist?**

Every product decision should be justifiable using the findings within this document.

---

# Industry Overview

The global events industry is one of the world's largest service industries.

Professional event businesses coordinate hundreds of interconnected activities for every event they deliver.

Typical activities include:

- Client communication
- Venue coordination
- Guest management
- Seating plans
- Catering
- Entertainment
- Photography
- Decorations
- Accommodation
- Transport
- Timelines
- Contracts
- Payments
- Health & Safety
- Staffing

Despite managing highly complex operations, the majority of businesses still rely on disconnected software and manual processes.

# Current Market Problems

## Problem One

### Information Fragmentation

Every event generates dozens of documents.

Examples include:

Guest List

Supplier List

Venue Requirements

Floor Plans

Menus

Running Orders

Contracts

Invoices

Risk Assessments

Each document evolves independently.

No single system guarantees users are viewing the latest version.

---

## Problem Two

### Administrative Overhead

Professional organisers spend a significant proportion of their working week performing repetitive administration.

Activities include:

Updating spreadsheets.

Creating revised PDFs.

Emailing suppliers.

Sending updated timelines.

Updating guest numbers.

Confirming dietary requirements.

These activities create operational cost without improving customer experience.

---

## Problem Three

### Collaboration Failure

Communication is largely asynchronous.

Updates are exchanged through:

Email

WhatsApp

Telephone

PDFs

Printed Documents

Information becomes duplicated.

Responsibility becomes unclear.

Version control disappears.

---

## Problem Four

### Poor Client Experience

Clients often feel disconnected from planning progress.

Questions such as:

"What is happening next?"

"Have suppliers confirmed?"

"Can I see the latest seating plan?"

require manual responses from organisers.

---

## Problem Five

### Supplier Coordination

Suppliers receive information from multiple sources.

Venue

Client

Planner

Emails

Telephone

Updated documents

Conflicting information introduces operational risk.

---

# Existing Software

Examples include:

Planning

CRM
